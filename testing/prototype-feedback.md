To assess the completeness of the prototype functionality (Patient and Administrator), we created a Google Form survey. The MyCare prototype survey received responses from 12 participants. Most of them were students (74.9%) and between the age of 18 and 24 (83.3%). The majority of participants schedule medical visits twice a year (58.3%), are tech-savvy (58.3%), and have made many online appointments scheduling. 75% of the 12 participants completed the appointment booking process with ease, while 25% required some effort.

Feature Ratings (1 – 5 Scale):
•	Browsing doctors – 3.42 / 5 (Lowest rated)
•	Selecting date & time – 4.17 / 5 (Highest rated)
•	Entering appointment details – 3.75 / 5
•	Viewing appointment – 3.83 / 5

Usability:
•	0% reported confusing elements
•	75% deemed the flow as reasonable or very logical
•	91.7% said there were no problems in regard to the buttons / features

Improvement Suggestions:
•	Reduce the number of steps in the process
•	Include a navigation back button
•	Fix the functionality of the cancel / confirm buttons

User Satisfaction:
•	Overall satisfaction – 4.08 / 5
•	Likelihood to use the system – 4.17 / 5

Conclusion:
With excellent task completion rates, high usability ratings and good market validation, more than 83% of participants said they would probably use the systems for actual appointments. The MyCare system got very positive feedback. Improving the display of doctor information and further optimising the booking procedure are important areas for development.
